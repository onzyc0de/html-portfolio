About the Band

Linkin Park was an American rock band formed in Agoura Hills, California, in 1996. The band became one of the most influential and successful rock acts of the 2000s and 2010s, known for blending nu-metal, alternative rock, electronic, and hip-hop elements.

Original Members:

  • Chester Bennington (vocals) – † 2017
  • Mike Shinoda (vocals, rapping, keyboards, guitar)
  • Brad Delson (guitar)
  • Dave "Phoenix" Farrell (bass)
  • Joe Hahn (turntables, sampling)
  • Rob Bourdon (drums)

Major Albums:

  1. Hybrid Theory (2000) – Their debut album, featuring hits like "In the End", "Crawling", and "One Step Closer". It became one of the best-selling albums of the 21st century.
  2. Meteora (2003) – Continued their success with songs like "Numb", "Somewhere I Belong", and "Faint".
  3. Minutes to Midnight (2007) – Marked a shift toward alternative rock, with "What I've Done" and "Shadow of the Day".
  4. A Thousand Suns (2010) – Experimental, concept-driven album with "The Catalyst" and "Waiting for the End".
  5. Living Things (2012) – Mixed electronic and rock, including "Burn It Down" and "Lost in the Echo".
  6. The Hunting Party (2014) – A return to heavier rock sounds.
  7. One More Light (2017) – A pop-oriented album featuring "Heavy" (with Kiiara).

Legacy & Impact:

  • Linkin Park sold over 100 million records worldwide.
  • They won 2 Grammy Awards (Best Hard Rock Performance for "Crawling" and Best Rap/Sung Collaboration for "Numb/Encore" with Jay-Z).
  • Known for their fusion of rock, rap, and electronic music, influencing many modern rock and metal bands.
  • Chester Bennington's powerful vocals and emotional lyrics resonated with fans dealing with mental health struggles.

Tragedy & Hiatus:

Chester Bennington died by suicide on July 20, 2017, leading to an indefinite hiatus for the band.

Mike Shinoda has pursued solo work and confirmed that Linkin Park has no current plans to continue without Chester.

Recent Developments:

  • In 2023, the band released a 20th-anniversary edition of Meteora, including the previously unreleased song "Lost", which became a hit.
  • The band remains active in honoring Chester’s legacy but has not announced any new music or tours.
